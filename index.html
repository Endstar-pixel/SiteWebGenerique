Home
Welcome to the Pokémon Trainer Hub, a fictional community portal where trainers can explore regions, compare teams, and discover new adventures. Everything on this page is written as a playful English example site for a Pokémon-inspired world.
Before joining this hub, I worked as a roaming field researcher in the Kanto and Johto regions, collecting data on wild encounters, evolution patterns, and type matchups. Today, the goal is to help trainers understand battle strategies, team building, and the hidden habits of Pokémon across every region.
My first big journey began in a small town with a single starter Pokémon, a worn backpack, and a notebook full of Pokédex sketches. The project focused on how trainers can track encounter patterns, build balanced teams, and turn everyday discoveries into useful battle knowledge.

    Education

  • [Champion Thesis] Battle Strategy on Functional Data (2017). pdf abstract

    In spring 2014, I completed a Master's Degree in Battle Statistics at the University of Pallet. Then I joined a research lab to build a Bayesian version of the FLiRTI method, producing sparse estimates for a trainer response model. A coefficient is considered sparse when it is zero on some intervals and has a simple shape such as a constant or piecewise linear pattern.

  • [Master Thesis] A Bayesian alternative to the FLiRTI model (2014).

    In spring 2013, during the second half of my Master's Degree in Battle Statistics, I completed a training course supervised by Professor Oak and Professor Elm, in collaboration with Daisy Oak and Bill. The goal was to explore a statistical link between the Glow Seed pattern and rare evolution hotspots.

  • [MSc Report] The influence of rare berry patterns on battle performance (2013).

    R package: Trainer Toolkit

    This fictional package is meant to help students and trainers learn R while tracking their battles, teams, and Pokédex notes.

    To install and use this package:

    • Clone the git repository from the URL https://example.com/trainer-toolkit
    • Install the package from the source file

    This package is under construction. The .tar.gz source may not always be available yet, and the functions may still be evolving.
